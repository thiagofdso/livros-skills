# Frame selection

Choose frames intentionally. Do not stack them mechanically.

## Core frames

### Intrigue frame
Use when:
- you need attention
- the audience is distracted
- the opening needs curiosity

Works well for:
- opening slides
- hidden pattern reveals
- surprising contrasts

### Prize frame
Use when:
- you want to position the opportunity as valuable
- you want to reduce neediness
- you want the audience to feel they are choosing into something important

Works well for:
- value slides
- offer slides
- future-state slides

### Time frame
Use when:
- a decision should not drift
- timing matters
- the window of opportunity is real

Works well for:
- why-now slides
- closing slides
- decision-path slides

### Moral authority frame
Use when:
- trust matters
- credibility is needed
- the audience is skeptical

Works well for:
- proof
- implementation confidence
- high-stakes proposals

### Power frame
Use carefully.
Use only when the context supports confident authority and the presenter can sustain it.

## Heuristics by situation
- executive room: intrigue + moral authority
- sales pitch: intrigue + prize + time
- internal buy-in: intrigue + why-now + proof
- interview: narrative + moral authority + prize
- technical proposal: intrigue + hidden risk + proof

## Rule
The opening frame should help the audience care before asking them to analyze.
