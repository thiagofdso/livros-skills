# Deck arc

Choose the deck size that fits the context.

## Formats
- Short pitch: 5–7 slides
- Standard deck: 8–12 slides
- Executive deep dive: 10–15 slides

Default to 8–10 slides unless the context clearly requires another format.

## Default arc

### Slide 1 — Reframe the room
Goal:
- seize attention
- define what the conversation is really about

Use:
- sharp tension
- bold contrast
- strong headline
- minimal text

Avoid:
- agenda-first
- biography-first
- feature-first

### Slide 2 — Put a human in the problem
Goal:
- activate narrative mode

Use:
- user, leader, team, customer, or real situation
- obstacle
- consequence
- concrete language

### Slide 3 — Why now
Goal:
- show why the old way is no longer enough

Use:
- market shift
- operational change
- cost pressure
- technology shift
- expectation shift

### Slide 4 — The insight
Goal:
- reveal the hidden pattern

Examples:
- the real bottleneck is not X, it is Y
- most teams are solving the wrong problem
- the issue is not effort, it is fragmentation

### Slide 5 — The solution
Goal:
- show the offer clearly

Use:
- one-line value proposition
- 3 blocks max
- simple visual or diagram

### Slide 6 — The prize
Goal:
- make the upside desirable

Use:
- business gain
- emotional relief
- speed
- visibility
- confidence
- growth
- strategic advantage

### Slide 7 — Proof
Goal:
- move into rational confidence

Use:
- metrics
- examples
- traction
- evidence
- mockups
- cases
- ROI

### Slide 8 — Decision path
Goal:
- make the next step concrete and easy

Use:
- what happens next
- timing
- expected commitment
- pilot or approval path

## Optional extra slides
Add only if useful:
- objections / FAQ
- rollout plan
- pricing / investment
- comparison
- implementation roadmap
