# Slide title patterns

Use slide titles that make a point, create tension, or reveal meaning.
Do not use neutral labels like “Overview”, “Solution”, or “Dashboard” unless the user explicitly wants a dry structure.

## Rules
- Make the title carry the argument.
- Prefer one idea per title.
- Use contrast, consequence, or insight.
- Keep titles short enough to scan quickly.
- Prefer concrete wording over abstraction.

## Strong title formulas

### 1. The real problem is not X, it is Y
Use when:
- the audience is focused on the wrong issue
- the presentation needs reframing

Examples:
- The real bottleneck is not effort, it is fragmentation
- The real problem is not growth, it is follow-through
- The real issue is not data, it is decision latency

### 2. Most people / teams / companies are doing X, but Y is what matters
Use when:
- you want to challenge common assumptions
- you want a contrarian but credible angle

Examples:
- Most teams optimize activity, but consistency is what drives outcomes
- Most churches track attendance, but discipleship is what sustains growth
- Most companies buy tools, but adoption is what creates value

### 3. What changed is X, so Y no longer works
Use when:
- you need a strong why-now slide
- environmental change matters

Examples:
- What changed is speed, so manual follow-up no longer works
- What changed is user expectation, so static training no longer works
- What changed is complexity, so isolated systems no longer work

### 4. Without X, Y becomes harder, slower, or more expensive
Use when:
- you need to make cost of inaction visible
- you want practical urgency

Examples:
- Without a unified view, pastoral follow-up becomes inconsistent
- Without automation, training becomes slower and harder to scale
- Without clear ownership, execution becomes expensive

### 5. This is where the current model breaks
Use when:
- you are exposing hidden failure points
- you want a strong transition into insight

Examples:
- This is where the current process breaks
- This is where manual coordination starts to fail
- This is where visibility disappears

### 6. The opportunity is bigger than it looks
Use when:
- you want to expand perceived upside
- the audience is underestimating strategic value

Examples:
- The opportunity is bigger than event management
- The opportunity is bigger than course delivery
- The upside is bigger than operational efficiency

### 7. The winning move is not more X, but better Y
Use when:
- the audience assumes the answer is more effort, budget, or control
- you want to pivot toward your mechanism

Examples:
- The winning move is not more meetings, but better follow-up
- The winning move is not more content, but better learning loops
- The winning move is not more tools, but better orchestration

### 8. Instead of X, do Y
Use when:
- you need clarity and directness
- the deck is tactical or executive

Examples:
- Instead of managing events in fragments, manage the journey end to end
- Instead of uploading content manually, generate learning automatically
- Instead of reacting late, detect needs early

### 9. X creates Y
Use when:
- you want causal clarity
- the audience needs a simple mechanism

Examples:
- Visibility creates better pastoral decisions
- Fast feedback creates better engagement
- Structured follow-up creates stronger retention

### 10. X is the difference between Y and Z
Use when:
- you want a memorable comparison
- the audience needs a sharp distinction

Examples:
- Follow-up is the difference between attendance and transformation
- Adoption is the difference between software and impact
- Timing is the difference between interest and action

### 11. If X continues, Y will happen
Use when:
- you want to make risk explicit
- the audience responds to consequences

Examples:
- If follow-up remains manual, visibility will keep shrinking
- If training stays fragmented, engagement will plateau
- If data stays isolated, decisions will stay reactive

### 12. The future state looks like this
Use when:
- you are transitioning into vision or solution
- you want to make the prize concrete

Examples:
- The future state is one journey, not scattered tools
- The future state is measurable discipleship, not guesswork
- The future state is automated learning with human guidance

## Title styles by slide role

### Opening slide
Use:
- tension
- hidden cost
- bold contrast
- provocative clarity

Examples:
- Growth without follow-up is fragile
- The problem is not activity. It is continuity.
- What looks organized is actually fragmented

### Why-now slide
Use:
- change
- pressure
- timing
- strategic window

Examples:
- What changed is not the mission. It is the scale.
- The old model cannot keep up with the new demand
- This becomes more expensive every quarter

### Insight slide
Use:
- hidden pattern
- misunderstanding
- reframing

Examples:
- The real bottleneck is between event and follow-up
- The gap is not content. It is continuity.
- Most teams are solving the visible symptom

### Solution slide
Use:
- new model
- direct contrast
- simple promise

Examples:
- One system connects event, learning, and care
- This turns scattered activity into a guided journey
- The platform is the coordination layer

### Proof slide
Use:
- confidence
- causal evidence
- clarity

Examples:
- The model works because it closes the feedback loop
- This is what measurable visibility looks like
- The value appears in speed, consistency, and action

### Closing slide
Use:
- decision
- next step
- momentum

Examples:
- The next move is a controlled pilot
- Start small. Measure fast. Expand with confidence.
- The goal is not discussion. It is a decision.

## Weak vs strong examples

Weak:
- Overview
- Problem
- Solution
- Benefits
- Dashboard
- Next steps

Strong:
- The real bottleneck is invisible until it is expensive
- Most teams are operating without continuity
- One layer connects the whole journey
- Visibility changes the quality of the response
- This is what leaders need to see in one place
- The right next step is a pilot, not a debate

## Rule
If the slide title could fit any deck on any topic, it is probably too weak.
