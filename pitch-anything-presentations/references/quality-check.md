# Quality check

Review the presentation before finalizing.

## Check strategy
- Is the audience clear?
- Is the desired outcome explicit?
- Is the core tension clear?
- Is the why-now credible?

## Check opening
- Does the first slide create tension or curiosity?
- Does the opening avoid dense detail?
- Is the deck avoiding agenda-first or feature-first opening?

## Check flow
- Does the deck move from frame to narrative to insight to solution to proof to decision?
- Is heavy analysis delayed until interest exists?
- Is each slide doing one clear job?

## Check persuasion
- Does the presentation address likely resistance?
- Is the value made desirable?
- Is there enough proof for confidence?
- Is the ending asking for a real next step?

## Check clarity
- Are slide titles making a point?
- Is the language concrete?
- Are visuals doing argumentative work?
- Are there too many bullets or too much text?

## Failure modes
Revise if you see:
- missing discovery
- assumed audience or objective
- biography-first opening
- feature dump too early
- proof too early
- weak CTA
- fake urgency
- manipulative tone
